Physical Health

Importance of Movement

Individuals of all ages should have regular activity to maintain good health. There are various benefits to movement, such as improving cardiovascular health, weight management, decreasing risk of chornic diseases, promoting mental well-being, and an increase in energy. Regular activity can also improve sleep quality and the quality of your life.

CDC Recommendations

The CDC recommends that adults aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity each week. Additionally, it is advised to incorporate muscle-strengthening activities at least two days a week.

Strategies

  • Schedule physical activity into your daily routine.
  • Choose activities that you enjoy.
  • Incorporate incidental physical activity, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or walking instead of driving short distances.
  • Take regular movement breaks throughout the day.
  • Find a workout buddy or join a group exercise class to add a layer of accountability.

Basics of Nutrition

Nutriont plays a key role in our health and well-being. A blance diet full of nutrients, vitamins, and minerals help keep our bodies functions. Focusing on balance and making informed choices is essential to a healthy diet.

Incorporate Whole Foods

Whole foods are unprocessed foods that have their natural nutrients, fiber, and antioxidants. Consuming whole grains over highly processed foods promote digestion and nutrient absorption. Since they are lower in added sugars, unhealthy fats, and sodium, incorporating whole foods into your diet can help manage weight, reduce risk of chronic diseases and enhance overall vitality.

Strategies

  • Prioritize fresh fruits and vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds in your meals and snacks.
  • Opt for lean proteins like poultry, fish, and plant-based sources like beans and tofu.
  • Minimize the consumption of processed foods, sugary beverages, and unhealthy fats.
  • Meal planning and prep to incorporate more whole foods in your diet.
  • Read food labels and look for sugar, sodium, and unhealthy fat content.
  • Mix up your meals with new recipes!

Importance of Hydration

Hydration is essential for overall health, as it plays an vital role in regulating body temperature, lubricating joings, transporting nutrients, and eliminatin waste. Lack of water consumption, dehyrdration, can lead to health issues, such as fatigue, headaches, and a decresase and physical performance.

Benefits of H20

Hydration has many benefits like promoting healthy digestion, proper organ function, and nutrient absorption. Keeping up on your H20 also supports cardivascular health by maintaining blood volume and circulation. Furthermore, hydration can help reulate body weight, ehnace metabolism, and contribute to health skin.

Strategies

  • Drink water regularly throughout the day, aiming for 3.7 liters for men and 2.7 liters for women.
  • Have a water bottle on hand to help remind you to drink throughout the day.

  • Limit the consumption of like alcohol, caffeinated drinks, and sugary sodas.

  • Listen to your body's signals of thirst (sometimes it likes to disguise itself as hunger).

Introduction to Stress

Stress is a natural response to the demands and pressures we encounter daily. There is acute and chronic stress, which both can impact phsyical and mental well-being. Signs of stress can include increased heart rate, irritability, difficulty concentrating, and more.

Impact of Stress

Stress affects various aspects of our health, such as physical health, emotional health, and social health. There is also a connection between stress and common health issues that can lead to chronic diseases, including weakened immune system, lack of sleep, and cardiovascular issues. Stress can also contribute to anxiety, depression, and lifestyle satisfaction.

Strategies

  • Be mindful of what is causing stress in your life.
  • Learn stress reduction techniques like breathwork, meditation, and exercise.
  • Understand the importance and how nutrition, sleep, self-care, and social support can impact stress.
  • Identify coping mechanisms that work for you, such as social support, time management, and setting SMART goals.
  • Ensure you incorporate stress management practices into your daily life to help promote resilience and well-being.

Introduction to Sleep

Sleep plays a vital role in our physical and mental well-being. There are different stages of sleep that have specific functions, such as REM and non-REM sleep. Having a consistent sleep schedule can impact circadian rhythms.

Benefits of Sleep

There are many benefits of sleep for our overall well-being, such as improving cognitive function, memory consolidation, and learning abilities. A consistent sleep routine can also help regulate mood, reduce stress, and promote hormonal balance.

Strategies

  • Create a space for success by making sure your sleep area is dark, quiet, and comfortable.
  • Go to bed and wakeup at a consistent time.
  • Limit the amount of screen time before bed.
  • Ensure that you are eating whole grains and have a health diet.
  • Practice relaxtion and stress management techniques to prepare for restful sleep.
  • Seek professional help if you believe that you may have a sleep disorder.

Mental Disorders

Mental disorders like anxiety and depression can have a great impact on mental health. Anxiety and depression have a high prevalence, and early identification can help with intervention and improved outcomes. Individuals should understand the risk and warning signs, such as increased/decreased appetite, excessive sleeping, racing thoughts, and elevated mood.

Managing Stress and Anxiety

Seeking professional help is essential when it comes to interventions for anxiety and depression. Many of them do promote evidence-based strategies, such as CBT and DBT. Relaxation techniques, like mindfulness and breathwork can also alleviate symptoms. However, seeking professional help and building a support network is important.

Promote Mental Well-being

  • Be aware of the signs and symptoms of anxiety and depression.
  • Make self-care and stress management important aspects of your daily routine.
  • Collect a variety of coping mechanisms that can manage and prevent mental disorders.
  • Show empathy, understanding, and create a supportive environment for others that struggle.
